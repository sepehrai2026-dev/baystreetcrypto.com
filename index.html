Guide

Getting started with crypto in Canada

Choose a registered Canadian exchange

Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) require crypto trading platforms to register. Using a registered exchange ensures your platform meets Canadian regulatory standards and your assets have some baseline protections.

Understand your CRA obligations

The CRA treats crypto as a commodity. Every trade, swap, and spend is a taxable event. Keep meticulous records from day one — retroactively tracking cost basis across multiple exchanges and wallets is a nightmare.

Consider crypto ETFs for simplicity

Canada pioneered spot Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs, available on the TSX. If you want crypto exposure without managing wallets and keys, ETFs in your TFSA or RRSP can be a tax-efficient option.

Explore mining opportunities

Canada's cheap hydroelectric power — especially in Quebec, Manitoba, and British Columbia — makes it one of the best countries in the world for Bitcoin mining. Understand the regulatory and energy landscape before investing in hardware.
